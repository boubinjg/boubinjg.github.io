NSF Graduate Research Fellowship

In 2019, I was lucky enough to receive the prestigeous NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. Per the NSF, The NSF Graduate Research Fellowship Program recognizes and supports outstanding graduate students in NSF-supported science, technology, engineering, and mathematics disciplines who are pursuing research-based Master's and doctoral degrees at accredited United States institutions.

The NSF GRFP provides a substantial stipend among other benefit over three years of graduate education. The GRFP is a great opportunity to earn external funding for your PhD and distinguish yourself from your peers. The application process is relatively straightforward. The NSF requires a 2 page research statement and 3 page personal statement which are judged based on the NSF's intellectual merit and broader impact criteria. The research statement proposes a course of research that you may undertake as a graduate student. Mine focused on work related to the SoftwarePilot project, much of which was either already underway but unpublished, or is currently being developed. The personal statement allows you to describe yourself as a researcher and a person. The NSF reviewers want to learn about you as a person, and this is your opportunity to tell them why you deserve their funding.
