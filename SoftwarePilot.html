Research


SoftwarePilot

With Dr. Chris Stewart, John Chumley, Naveen T.R. Babu, Shiqi Zhang, Zichen Zhang,
and many more

My primary work as a PhD student at OSU focuses on Fully Autonomous Aerial Systems (FAAS). Our work on FAAS encompases diverse domains: surveying crop fields for diseases and pests, searching disaster areas, and autonomous photography. This work has generated a series of publictions and presentations, and was the focus of my NSF GRFP research statement. While at OSU, members of the ReRout Lab and I have developed SoftwarePilot, an FAAS middleware that we use to turn consumer UAVs and edge systems into FAAS.

SoftwarePilot is fully open source, and can be found on github here. SoftwarePilot has been used in a series of papers published by members of ReroutLab. We have had a poster and extended abstract at IoTDI 2018, a paper at ICAC 2019, and a paper at SEC 2019, all of which are available for download below. Throughout this research we have collected a series of UAV collected image datasets. Datasets include UAV captures of human faces, and UAV scans of Ohio corn fields. These datasets are available for download on the SoftwarePIlot website.
