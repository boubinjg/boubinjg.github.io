Resume

I am currently an incoming PhD student in computer science at Ohio State University working with Dr. Christopher Stewart in the ReRout lab. My research will focus on high performance autonomous unmanned aerial vehicles, specifically applied to agricultural. My present and past work falls mainly in the fields of unmanned aerial systems, high performance computing, automation, simulation, and computer systems as a whole.

I received a B.S in Computer Science from Miami University in May of 2017. While studying at Miami, I worked with Dr. Dhananjai Rao creating efficient process parallel agent based simulations. I later worked for the United States Department of Defense at the Air Force Institute of Technology under Maj. Christina Rusnock, PhD. Under Maj. Rusnock, I worked on multiple projects relating to discrete event simulation, automation theory, and system design. I also worked for Dr. Michael Miller, creating testing and evaluation software for unmanned aerial systems.

I prefer C/C++ development, but can also use Java, Python and a number of other languages. I am skilled with algorithms and data structures, and I love to write concurrent code. I have experience with compiler design, machine learning (particularly in the realm of data analysis), and artificial intelligence. I like to use these skills to solve novel, interdisciplinary research problems.
